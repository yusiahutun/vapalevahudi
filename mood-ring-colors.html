14 Mood Ring Colors And Their Meanings

Since they first gained popularity in the 1970s, mood rings and their colors, primarily how they change by way of reading our emotions, have become a subject of fascination to people for what is now half a century later. In the modern world, they have become a nostalgic symbol of self-expression, with many wondering what each mood ring color represents.

To that end, in this article we will delve into the world of mood rings and explore the meanings behind 14 (though numbers may vary depending on design) different colors, from passionate reds to calming blues, and what each hue carries as its unique message about our emotional state. Whether you own a mood ring or are simply curious about the psychology behind these color-changing gems, join us as we unravel the secrets hidden within these tiny, captivating circles.

1. Black

Black in the mood ring color chart is associated with a state of deep and intense emotions, leaning more towards a dark and heavy mood, and often associated with feelings of sadness, grief, or despair. So, when someone's mood ring turns black, it indicates that they are experiencing overwhelming emotions, often negative, and/or going through a difficult time in their life.

Not only does this color reflect a sense of heaviness, darkness and turmoil in one's emotional state, but as a result it suggests a need for support, understanding, and empathy from outside the self, usually in the form of family, friends or lover. Basically, it serves as a visual representation of the individual's internal struggles and a reminder both the wearer, and others, to be compassionate and sensitive towards their emotional well-being.

2. Gray

If you're wondering what does the color gray mean on a mood ring, it typically signifies a neutral or balanced state of emotions. As gray is often associated with calmness, stability, and a sense of composure, this color showing up on a mood ring suggests that the wearer is neither particularly happy nor sad, but rather in a state of equilibrium, without leaning towards any extreme.

A gray color on the mood ring may also indicate a person's ability to handle stress or difficult situations with ease, suggesting a level-headedness along with an ability to adapt to whatever circumstances they might find themselves in life, work, or relationships. However, a more positive interpretation of this color showing up can indicate that, while the wearer may not possess or display any intense emotions, they have achieved a sense of contentment and peace within oneself, almost akin to enlightenment.

3. Brown

In terms of mood ring colors and what they mean, brown represents a sense of stability, grounding, practicality, and is often associated with feelings of security, reliability, and a down-to-earth nature in the wearer. Brown is a warm and comforting color, reminiscent of the soil, nature, and other earthy characteristics, all promoting a sense of calmness and reassurance.

When the mood ring turns brown, it suggests that the wearer is feeling centered, balanced, and in control of their emotions, whether in life, work, and/or relationships, while also signifying a state of being grounded, physically or mentally, and connected to the present moment. Brown indicates a practical and realistic approach to life, emphasizing the need for stability and reliability, and overall, the color brown on a mood ring teaches us the importance of finding stability and security in our emotions.

4. Amber

Next up in our list of what do the colors of a mood ring mean is Amber which represents a combination of emotions and states of mind, but is most often associated with feelings of caution, alertness, and being grounded. Amber signifies a sense of clarity and balance, indicating a calm and focused state of being.

It suggests that the person wearing it is observant and thoughtful, aware of their surroundings as well as the emotions within themselves, while also indicating that the person is experiencing a mix of emotions, neither overly positive nor negative, making it more dynamic than the previous colors on our list. However, above all, it symbolizes a state of being in control, self-assured, and ready to face challenges, and overall, amber as a color in your mood ring signifies a harmonious and centered mood.

5. Green

The color green when it comes to mood rings signifies calmness, tranquility, and balance while also representing a state of emotional well-being and harmony, especially with nature. Given how everpresent green is in terms of the outdoors, one can understand that it also reflects a peaceful and relaxed mood, indicating a person's contentment, serenity, and is associated with feelings of growth, renewal, and rejuvenation.

Famously associatied with the ancient tradition of Celtic mood rings, the color's meaning holds additional significance in that culture because for them, green symbolizes nature, fertility, and rebirth while also representing the lush landscapes of Ireland centering on the interconnectedness between humans and the natural world. Green in Celtic mood rings is believed to enhance spiritual and emotional connections, fostering a sense of grounding and connection to the Earth.

6. Aqua

Aqua, as a mood ring color stands for a calm and peaceful emotional state as this particular shade of blue-green represents tranquility, serenity, and harmony with the wearer of an aqua mood ring likely to be in a state of relaxation and contentment. It suggests that the individual is experiencing a sense of emotional balance and stability.

When this color shows up on the wearer's mood ring, it reflects that the person is in touch with their emotions, able to express themselves calmly, and possesses a deep (like the ocean) sense of inner peace, though not particularly stability. More likely, this color indicates that the wearer may be going through a period of emotional healing or is naturally inclined towards a peaceful disposition; also suggests that the wearer is open-minded and adaptable person who has a positive outlook on life.

7. Blue

The color blue appearing in a mood ring represents a specific emotional state often associated with feelings of calmness, tranquility, and serenity, indicating that the wearer is experiencing a relaxed and peaceful state of being. It further suggests a sense of contentment and harmony as well as a deep sense of introspection and contemplation, and is often linked with a person's ability to express themselves and communicate with others effectively.

When a mood ring turns blue, it might suggest that the wearer is in a thoughtful and reflective state, but not pensive, and perhaps even engaging in self-analysis or considering their emotions and thoughts. It also shows that the wearer is feeling dependable, trustworthy, and committed while suggesting that not only are they are in a reliable, faithful mood, but open to providing support and understanding to those around them.

8. Indigo or Dark Blue

In terms of mood rings and their color meanings, the indigo or dark blue color represents a deep and intense state of mind, and is often associated with feelings of calmness, introspection, and spiritual awareness. Indigo signifies a profound level of thought and contemplation, suggesting that the wearer is deeply engrossed in their own emotions and thoughts.

This color can also indicate a heightened sense of intuition and psychic abilities, which is what separates it from its more lighter counterpart, as the introspection here is more active than passive, and also supernatural. Overall, indigo or dark blue in mood rings reflects a person who is not only in touch with their innermost self, but also constantly seeking clarity and understanding in their emotional and mental state and wellbeing.

9. Violet

In the mood ring colors meaning list, violet represents a deep and intense emotional state, and is often associated with feelings of mystery, spirituality, and introspection. When a mood ring turns violet, it signifies that the wearer is experiencing profound emotions and is possibly going through a period of self-reflection, while simultaneously suggesting a complex and intricate mental state, where the person may be exploring deeper layers of their thoughts and emotions.

Violet also hints at a sense of creativity and imagination, with the wearer probably engaging in some form of artistic or intellectual pursuits, as their mind is perhaps inclined towards abstract thinking and exploring unconventional ideas. Overall, the presence of violet on a mood ring indicates a rich and intricate emotional landscape within the wearer, characterized by depth, spirituality, and a quest for self-discovery.

10. Pink

The color Pink in mood rings is a delicate shade that evokes a sense of tranquility and calmness, while representing a peaceful and contented mood, where one feels at ease with themselves and the world around them. This color, in its lighter shades, also signifies affection, tenderness, and love, and is often associated with feelings of warmth, compassion, and nurturing.

On the other hand, when the Pink color deepens or becomes more vibrant, it may indicate heightened emotions such as excitement, enthusiasm, or passion, with this surge of energy indicating a lively mood and an outgoing attitude. This vibrant shade can also signify a sense of playfulness and joy, indicating a person's readiness to engage in fun and social activities, thus underlying how multifaceted this color, and its many shades, can be.

11. Red

When it comes to the colors of a mood ring, the color red carries a multifaceted meaning that encompasses a wide range of emotions, but is often associated with strong and intense feelings, symbolizing passion, love, and desire. Red also signifies power, energy, and assertiveness, reflecting a sense of confidence and ambition.

However, red can also evoke negative emotions like anger, aggression, and frustration, and sometimes represents itself as a warning sign, indicating intense emotions that need to be not only acknowledged but also managed. In terms of mood ring colors, red can indicate a heightened state of arousal or excitement, but it is essential to consider the context and individual interpretation as its multifaceted nature reminds us that emotions are complex and can fluctuate, requiring mindfulness and self-awareness to navigate effectively.

12. Yellow

When the color yellow appears on a wearer's mood ring, it typically represents happiness, joy, and optimism, and is associated with feelings of positivity, cheerfulness, and contentment. Furthermore, this particular color on a mood ring may indicate that the wearer is experiencing a sense of well-being, enthusiasm, and a general sense of satisfaction with their current situation, be it in life, work and/or relationships.

It also suggests that the wearer is feeling upbeat, energetic, and hopeful, because yellow is often linked to a sunny disposition, symbolizing warmth, and a bright outlook. So, to surmise, the person whose mood ring is showing yellow, no matter what shade, is most likely to be in a state of mental clarity, as well as creativity, with an open, optimistic, and hopeful mindset, shining bright as the sun, so to speak.

13. Orange

In terms of mood rings, the color orange typically symbolizes enthusiasm, energy, and creativity, with a strong association towards feelings of excitement, passion, and a sense of adventure. As with yellow, orange is often seen as a warm and vibrant color, representing a positive and outgoing mood.

When a mood ring turns orange, it suggests that the wearer is feeling inspired, motivated, and ready to take on new challenges while also indicating a state of heightened emotions, optimism, and a desire for social interaction. Additionally, orange is commonly associated with adventure and stimulation, and signifies a zest for life and a willingness to embrace new experiences so when the mood ring displays orange, it suggests that the wearer is open to exploring their surroundings and seeking out opportunities for personal growth and fulfillment.

14. White or Clear

As with the earlier disccused color gray, when a mood ring displays a white or clear color, it typically signifies a neutral or balanced emotional state while also implying that the wearer is experiencing a calm and peaceful mood with a sense of clarity, contentment and equilibrium. This color, or lack thereof, indicates a state of emotional stability and mental tranquility, where the individual feels relaxed and at ease.

The white or clear color in a mood ring often suggests a lack of strong emotions, such as anger, excitement, or sadness as well as indicating a state of neutrality or detachment, where the wearer is neither overly happy nor sad. Overall, the white or clear color in a mood ring suggests a serene and composed disposition, reflecting a harmonious and centered emotional state similar to a Buddha-like level of emotionlessness.
