Kate Middleton admitted she regretted Christmas Day outfit in candid moment with fan

A fan, who has photographed many members of the royal family, has shared a remark Kate Middleton made about her Christmas Day “regret.”

Karen Anvil, who is an amateur photographer and fan of the royal family, has opened up about getting up close and personal with Kate Middleton during the Christmas period. She said she gave Princess Charlotte a doll and said Kate Middleton was talking to her daughter and shared a “regret” she had for Christmas Day.

Kate Middleton regret she had on Christmas Day

During their exchange, Kate, 41, explained she felt “too hot” in what she had decided to wear and admitted she “shouldn’t have worn” her grey woolen coat.

Karen told Metro: “When they get past us and they’re with the press, they’re very different. The pictures that you see with them when they leave the church are all quite serious, it’s because they’ve got their press face on.

“Kate was talking to my daughter about clothes and how she was feeling too hot, she said ‘I really shouldn’t have worn this.’ I’m there talking to Charlotte about dolls and my daughter’s talking about fashion with Kate.”

Kate and her fashion

There are other instances in which Kate Middleton might regret what she was wearing. It was claimed one time she was having a ‘dig’ at her sister-in-law. However, a royal expert was quick to say the idea that she would do that to Meghan Markle was “ridiculous.”

Kate and other female members of the royal family wore similar bright colors to Kate’s Together at Christmas carol service at Westminster Abbey. This came after the premiere of Prince Harry and Meghan documentary Harry & Meghan. In the Netflix limited series, the 42-year-old claimed she would “tone down” what she wore in public appearances and wear colors like white and beige so not as to overshadow her in-laws.

The former TV actress added: “Until that last week in the UK, I rarely wore color and I never wanted to upstage or ruffle any feathers, so I just tried to blend in.”

All of the female royals in attendance at the carol service were dressed in burgundy-toned colors, not neutral.
