Where the Rising Damp cast are now - from Harry Potter films to tragic deaths

Rising Damp followed the lives of a self-centred landlord and his unfortunate tenants in a rundown Victorian townhouse.

The beloved British bedsit sitcom, written by Eric Chappell, first graced our TV screens back in 1974 and was adapted from his 1973 stage play called The Banana box.

The rugged ITV comedy was set in Yorkshire (most likely Leeds) and ran for four years until its final episode on May 8, 1978.

However, after this the popular tenant-sitcom was then revived when it was adapted into a feature film of the same name in 1980.

And us Brits clearly loved the landlord show, because Rising Damp topped the list as the highest-ranking ITV show in BBC's 100 Best Sitcoms poll back in 2004.

As today (May 9) marks 43 years since Rising Damp's final episode aired, to mark the occasion, Daily Star have decided to take a trip down memory lane and see what the cast are up to now.

Richard Beckinsale (who played Alan Moore)

Richard Beckinsale, who is also the father of acclaimed actress Kate Beckinsale, played Alan, the long-haired, left-wing medical student.

In Rising Damp, he was often the butt of the jokes from his landlord, Rigsby.

In 1979, after Rising Damp ended, Alan was filming a new sitcom called Bloomers which was set in a florist's shop.

He'd also just wrapped up work on the the film of Porridge, and was even taking on a more serious role by playing a detective in Bloody Kids, the Stephen Frears film.

However, tragically the adored actor passed away after suffering a massive heart attack on March 19, the same year.

It was only three days later that Sydeney Lotterby, the producer of Porridge, won a BAFTA award with comedy legend Ronnie Barker.

Ronnie had said at the time: "The death of my friend Richard Beckinsale has robbed me of the joy of this award but the pride of winning it still remains."

Kate Beckinsale has also said she's found it "extremely comforting" to watch her father in his old TV shows.

Leonard Rossiter (who played Rupert Rigsby)

Next up is the famous bully landlord of the series, Leonard Rossiter.

Soon after playing Rigsby in the show, he appeared a couple of years later in The Fall And Rise of Reginald Perrin's first series, but was mostly known for stand-alone drama roles on TV such as Thirty-Minute Theatre and Play Of The Month.

However, Rossiter then reprised his iconic landlord role for the 1980 feature film adaptation of Rising Damp, having played the same role on the stage and on television.

He continued to make a string of film appearances, such as in Britannia Hospital (1982), and even turned his hand to Shakespeare for the 1984 BBC adaptation of The Life and Death of King John.

His last film appearance would be in Dick Clement's 1985 comedy, Water, set on a fictional Caribbean island and British colony of Cascara.

Leonard died at the age of 57 on October 5, 1984 of a heart condition just before he was about to go on stage at the Lyric Theatre in London, as part of the production of Loot.

Don Warrington, who played the suave Philip Smith in the bedsit sitcom, shot to fame during the show.

The Trinidadian-born British actor now has an MBE, after receiving the formal honour in 2008.

Not just known for his Yorkshire sitcom role though, he went on to star as a regular in the crime drama, C.A.T.S. Eyes as government contact Nigel Beaumont until 1987, as well as playing Judge Ken Winyard in legal drama New Street Law.

You can also find him in programmes like Red Dwarf, Lovejoy, Manchild, and even as a Time Lord in several Doctor Who audio plays.

Warrington has found big success on the theatre circuit by performing with the National Theatre, Royal Shakespeare Company, the Royal Exchange and Bristol's Old Vic.

Warrington has also spoken out about the struggles of finding work as a black actor in the 1970s.

He told Coventry Live: "Of course, I had to make choices about the sort of roles I played but I’d say that I never took parts that were shameful or that ever degraded black people.

"These days, it’s better. There is more visibility for black actors."

Warrington took to the nation's dance floor when he competed in the sixth series of Strictly Come Dancing, making it to Week 5 after losing the dance-off against Heather Small.

And most recently, you'll find him as Commissioner Selwyn Patterson on BBC detective series, Death in Paradise.

Frances de la Tour (who played Ruth Jones)

Olivier Award-winning stage actress Frances de la Tour played the romantic college administrator Miss Ruth Jones.

A veteran of the stage, she has not just bagged three Olivier Award wins but also a Tony Award for her performance as Mrs Lintott in The History Boys, both in London and on Broadway.

Before reprising her History Boys role in the 2006 film adaptation, she became widely known for her role in the intentionally-loved Harry Potter franchise.

In her role as Madame Olympe Maxime in Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire, she played the headmistress of Beauxbatons, the French wizarding school, during the Triwizard Tournament.

Frances then reprised her fantastic fantasy role in Deathly Hallows - Part 1.

And you'll soon find her taking the reigns as Her Majesty, Queen Elizabeth II in the upcoming HBO Max show, The Prince.
