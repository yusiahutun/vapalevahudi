20 hip-hop female 2000s fashion trends worth exploring today

In the early 2000s, hip-hop broke through the music scene and made its mark in fashion. Hip-hop female 2000s fashion trends were just as iconic as the grunge fashion of the '90s. Everything that signaled affluence, from in-vogue clothing to designer jewelry, was iconic.

Hip-hop female 2000s fashion has been a trendsetter in the fashion world. From blinged-out grillz to trucker hats to the iconic Air Force 1s, some of the best 2000s hip-hop fashion trends continue to influence fashion on the streets and Paris runways.

20 hip-hop female 2000s fashion trends

Female hip-hop artists have had a big impact on the fashion industry. Hip-hop 2000s hip-hop fashion brands such as Von Dutch, Lacoste, and Sean John collaborated with musicians to advertise their products. According to Cosmopolitan, these are some 2000 hip-hop trends worth exploring today.

2. Fishnets

It-girls are layering fishnet tights under ripped jeans; bonus if the tops peek out over your waistband or you wear fishnet socks with sneakers or heels. Ask Gigi Hadid or any of her friends. While the light-coloured lenses may not do much to block the sun, they are Instagram selfie gold.

Although bucket hats are nothing new, you can make yours more interesting by adding patches or rhinestones. The studded embellishment everywhere, even on a cute bucket hat to make it feel more casual, makes this pea green shirt perfectly work even though it shouldn't.

5. Branded tracksuits

Early 2000s fashion can be summed up in one word, "JUICY," which was decorated in rhinestones on the but*ts of pop stars and hotel heiresses everywhere. As seen on Britney, Beyoncé, J. 6. Trucker hats

Justin Timberlake may have started this craze, but many women added a trucker hat to their ensemble to make it more relaxed on the red carpet. It's wonderful to see good ol' Gwen here.

7. Cardigans

While suburbanites' use of thrift stores increased in the 2000s, dressing like your grandfather gained popularity. Cardigans turned your grandparents into one of the most influential fashion icons, from the decline of emo music's influence on the public to the emergence of hipster culture at the end of the 2000s.

8. Low-rise jeans

Most people got a little ahead of themselves and demanded the low-rise jean's comeback as early as 2015. However, a few years later, after a decade of rambling rises, consider warming up to the concept of its reappearance.

9. Oversized belts

During her appearance at the Super Bowl 2001, Beyoncé nailed the 2000s fashion trifecta: low-rise, cropped pirate trousers with an enormous belt. Extra credit for the wallet chain.

10. Emo hair

The rise of pop-punk and the Vans Warped Tour in the middle of the decade spawned these shaggy haircuts. It was the introduction of Justin Bieber back in 2008 that sealed the style into popular culture. Either way, the shaggy, bangs-heavy haircut became such a cultural icon that it was immortalized on Bieber's wax statue.

11. Fedoras

Before class was for men and swag was for boys, fedoras had a much different reputation. Around the time fitted was hitting peak popularity, retro headwear was gracing both women's and men's heads with a more classic style.

12. Messenger bags

For all the love backpacks have today, messenger bags were the motion back in the aughts. As fixie bike culture began to swell, the bag of choice for bike messengers everywhere came right with it. While their practicality has stayed the same, the lack of people adopting bike messenger positions left people opting for the easier, more universal backpack instead.

13. Hipsters

If your jeans weren't wide-legged, were you wearing jeans in the 2000s? Everyone from backpack rappers to indie rockers had at least one pair of hipsters.

14. Throwback jerseys

Mitchell and Ness made a killing outfitting men in retro jerseys and fitted, reviving old-school logos in such a big way that by the time Allen Iverson was dominating the NBA, it was nearly impossible not to see him without something retro-related. The style became too oversaturated way too quickly, but there's no denying that this trend helped spawn the customizer movement seen today.

15. Folded t-shirt

To get this pop star-approved style, all you needed was your baggy t-shirt. You would turn it into a crop top with one hand below your shoulder with a few easy folds. The style can be complemented with a bucket hat or any other type of hat.

16. Oversize trousers

Women and men in the 2000s were rocking baggy clothing. The outfit was purportedly first worn in the 1980s and 1990s by the rap group Kriss Kross. Its popularity grew steadily and peaked in the 2000s. Rappers like P Diddy, Cam'ron, and Dipset helped to popularize the genre at the time.

17. Camouflage pants

They were also known as camo pants, and both men and women wore them during the 2000s. The outfits enhanced various wearing styles and gave the performers a jungle-like appearance. Males still wear the garments, but ladies like the feminine versions.

18. Bandana

In the 1980s and 1990s, most rappers made a fashion statement by donning bandanas, particularly Tupac Shakur, who frequently wore one. Some celebrities still wear the outfit now, which was one of the major fashion fads in the 2000s. Both men and women now cover their hands, neck, and heads with bandana scarves. The bandana is another early 2000s hip-hop fashion trend to try out in 2022.

19. Crop tops

Hip-hop 2000s fashion for black girls is here to stay. Although these clothes have been around for a while, the early 2000s hip-hop trend made them popular. Female artists wore various outfit designs in a variety of hues and materials. Surprisingly, supporters of other musical genres, like soul and pop music, also appreciated the ensemble.

20. Vests

2000s hip-hop fashion trends like the folded off-shoulder tops have become more popular among fashion fans. Ciara's appearance at the VH1 Save the Music event in 2006 serves as the ideal illustration of the vest-as-shirt trend that was popular in the 2000s, either worn over a bra top or tee.
