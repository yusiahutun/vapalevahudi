Kenya Defence Forces (KDF): ranks, salaries, and allowances

The Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) was established in 2010 under the Ministry of Defense. The military force constitutes the Kenya Army, Kenya Navy, and the Kenya Air Force. This article will help you understand the KDF salary rates in 2024.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

KDF is in charge of the defense and protection of Kenya's sovereignty and territorial integrity. Recruitment to the forces is done annually, and eligible candidates are between 26 and 34 years. The Salaries and Remuneration Commission of Kenya (SRC) reviews the Kenya Army salary ranges. The highest KDF payslip reads slightly above Ksh. 1 million.

KDF salary and allowances by rank

Kenya army ranks and salariesare based on the candidates' level of education and years of experience, among other factors. The KDF salaries are evaluated every four years, and SRC did the last review in 2017.

KDF officers enjoy handsome allowances while on peace missions. Those living in the barracks enjoy several benefits for army employees, like buying commodities at half the market price. KDF specialist officers are mostly graduates. They enter the military service with underlying training in various professions. A KDF specialist officer's salary differs from the rest of the officers, depending on the rank and one's specialization.

Kenya Army ranks

Kenya Defense Forces operate in ranks and are highly disciplined. They are in two main groups, Commissioned Officers and Non-commissioned Officers. The KDF Commissioned Officers are above the Non-commissioned Officers.

Commissioned Officers make crucial decisions and plans, while Non-commissioned Officers lead and train troops. These two main Kenya Defense Force ranks are split further into several sub-groups. Promotion is strictly based on merit and not one's service affliction.

KDF Non-commissioned officers (lowest to highest)

They are otherwise referred to as NCOs. These are military officers who have not pursued a commission. They earn their positions of authority by promotion through the enlisted ranks. Below are their ranks from lowest to highest:

  • Private Officer: These are KDF soldiers who have completed their first Phase of military training. They are at the lowest rank of non-commissioned officers.
  • Lance Corporal: A KDF Private Officer becomes a Lance Corporal after three years of serving the Private Officer position or passes Phase 2 training. The Lance Corporal manages a team of around four soldiers. The team is called a Section.
  • Corporal: It is the third-lowest rank of non-commissioned officers. A KDF soldier attains it after six-eight years of military service, and promotion depends on one's leadership skills. A Corporal manages a bigger number of soldiers than the Lance Corporal. Someone in this position also oversees military equipment like tanks and guns.
  • Sergeant: A KDF soldier gets this position after twelve years of good military service. A Sergeant commands a Platoon (35 soldiers) and advises the junior officers.
  • Senior Sergeant: There are two types of Senior Sergeant positions; a Staff or Color Sergeant. One gets to either position after working for two years as a Sergeant. A Senior Sergeant commands an Army company (around 120 soldiers).
  • Warrant Officer II: A Warrant Officer Class 2 trains, promotes, and disciplines a group of around 120 soldiers. One is also a senior adviser to a Major. A Major commands a sub-unit.
  • Warrant Officer I: A KDF soldier attains this position after 18 years of brilliant military service. A Warrant Officer Class 1 commands up to 650 officers and soldiers.

KDF Commissioned officers (lowest to highest)

These administrative KDF soldiers start as cadets and move up the ranks. They train at Kenya Military Academy (KMA) in Lanet, Nakuru County. Below are the ranks of KDF Commissioned Officers from lowest to highest:

  • Officer Cadet: This is a rank the officers hold immediately after training in Lanet. They are junior officers among the commissioned officers.
  • Second Lieutenant: Officers hold this position after two years of graduation from KMA. They can have the rank for up to three years while undergoing specialized training. You will lead up to 30 soldiers in a troop or platoon in this rank, both in operations and training.
  • Lieutenant: Officers attain this rank after serving for three to five years. They will be in the position for three years with an opportunity to gain specialized skills from your unit. Afterward, they will lead up to 30 soldiers in a troop or platoon.
  • Captain: Officers who attain this rank must have served for up to six years and will be second in command for up to 120 soldiers in a sub-unit. They are part of decision-making and planning processes with on-ground tactical responsibility for operations, logistic support, maintenance of equipment, and workforce.
  • Major: Officers gain promotion to this rank after 8 - 10 years of outstanding service and leadership skills. They get to command a sub-unit of about 120 soldiers and officers. Majors are responsible for their sub-unit's administration, welfare, and training (both in operations and the camps) and for managing their equipment.
  • Lieutenant Colonel: Officers command 4 to 5 sub-unit (about 650 soldiers and officers). A Lieutenant Colonel only holds the office for two years.
  • Colonel: As a colonel, the officer is not a field commander but the lowest of the staff ranks. The KDF staff will be senior operational advisors to the senior officers and serve as staff officers between brigade or battalion-level field commands.
  • Brigadier: As a brigadier, the KDF staff will be a field officer in charge of a brigade or a staff director.
  • Major General: A Major General is responsible for divisions and senior staff appointments. They are in charge of promotions in the military and are, at times, referred to as two-star generals. Major Generals can be promoted to service commanders.
  • Lieutenant General: Lieutenant General is one of the highest military ranks in Kenya. In this cadre, officers will be in charge of forming sizes for the Corps.
  • General: The General is the highest-ranking soldier and the chief of defense forces, while the private is the junior-most rank in the army. The General holds the positions of the Vice Chief of Defense Staff, Chief of Defense Staff, or General Staff. Only one person can be the General at a time.

Kenyan Air Force ranks and salaries

The Republic of Kenya established the Kenyan Air Force on 1 June 1964, immediately after independence, with the UK's assistance. KAF's headquarters is at Moi Air Base in Eastleigh, Nairobi. It has 152 aircraft, primarily operating from Laikipia Air Base in Nanyuki. Others are Forward Operating Base (FOB) Mombasa (Moi International Airport ), FOB Wajir, FOB Mandera, and FOB Nyeri.

Kenyan Navy ranks and salaries

The Republic of Kenya established the Kenyan Navy in 1964. Its headquarters is in Mombasa, with 18 vessels of different classes. The main naval base is in Mtongwe base in Mombasa (it covers Shimoni, Malindi, Msambweni, and Kilifi), and another is in Manda, Lamu.

These are the top two highest ranks in KDF.

Do KDF pay tax?

Kenya's military officers are not exempt from paying taxes, but most allowances are not taxed. They are only exempted from paying excise duty.
