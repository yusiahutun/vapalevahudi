NTSA exam: Dates, booking, and how to check your results

Have you completed a driving course and are confident with your knowledge? If yes, it’s time to apply for an NTSA exam. This exam takes place at NTSA offices nearest your driving school. For those within Nairobi, it can be conducted in NTSA offices in the Industrial area or an approved police station.

The National Transport and Safety Authority (NTSA) was established to bring sanity to Kenyan roads. NTSA is responsible for transport licensing, motor vehicle registration and inspection, road safety, driver testing and some traffic law enforcement.

NTSA exam booking fee

The NTSA exam booking fee is Ksh. 1050 and can be paid online through eCitizen NTSA. If you have yet to open an eCitizen account, don't fret – you can do it in seconds. All you need is your ID number.

NTSA exam dates

When booking for a driving test, you must state the date, time and location where you'd like to do it. Upon payment of the booking fee, you'll be given an NTSA driving test booking certificate.

All driving tests happen at Driving Test Units, which are based nationwide. The tests are conducted by a driving test examiner (DTE), and they test the applicants:

  • Knowledge of road signals and signs
  • Knowledge of road rules
  • Knowledge of any authorised highway or road code
  • Physical fitness to handle a motor vehicle of the class for which the license is needed

NTSA driving test results

The results are usually released about 12 hours after doing the test—log in to your NTSA eCitizen to check – if it's written "Licensed", you've passed.

You'll be given a certificate of competence (C of C) indicating the motor class or classes you're permitted to drive. The C of C is only valid for three months. Upon expiry, the licensing officer decides whether you'll undergo another driving test or, on the contrary, approve the application for driving license payment.

How is the NTSA driving test?

The NTSA exam, though crucial for obtaining a driver's license in Kenya, is easy, especially for well-prepared candidates. The exam is structured in two stages and is designed to evaluate theoretical knowledge and practical driving skills.

Driving test theory

Assessing the theoretical knowledge allows authorities to gauge your understanding of traffic laws, road signs and regulations. This knowledge is the foundation of safe driving behaviour. The test takes about five minutes, and you must pass it before taking the second one.

Driving test practical

The NTSA practical driving test assesses your ability to apply theoretical knowledge in real-world scenarios. It evaluates your actual driving skills and road behaviour.

This hands-on assessment allows the examiner to observe how well you can handle a vehicle, navigate traffic situations, execute manoeuvres such as lane changes and parking and reacting appropriately to various hazards and road conditions.

What happens when you fail the NTSA driving test?

It's possible to re-sit as an applicant if you fail the test. You can undergo a refresher training at a driving school before taking the re-test.

Note: the re-test registration fee is Ksh. 1050 and is processed through the eCitizen portal. NTSA doesn't accept cash payments. The re-test results will be uploaded and verified through your account within 24 hours of administration.

FAQs about NTSA

  • What is the pass mark for the NTSA driving test? For a learner to be considered successful, they must attain an 80% pass mark after compiling the average performance in both theoretical and practical units.
  • How to know if you've passed the NTSA test? NTSA test results are made available after approximately 12 hours after the examination. Access the NTSA eCitizen portal and check for the status "Licenced"; this indicates successful test completion.
  • Are NTSA exams hard? Don't worry; the questions on the NTSA Model Town Board exam aren't tricky. Furthermore, familiarizing yourself with similar tests will enhance your likelihood of success in the NTSA exams.
  • How many points do you need for NTSA DL? The smart DL is fitted with a chip that holds the driver's information, and a points-based system is used to encourage discipline among motorists. Each card has 100 points deducted every time the holder commits any traffic offence.
  • What is the next step after passing the NTSA test? After passing the NTSA test, one can apply for a driver's license. The entire process can be done online through the e-citizen portal.
  • Does NTSA work on Saturdays? Following the updated schedule, NTSA banking halls and offices operate Monday through Friday, from 7:30 am to 6:00 pm.
  • How does a person check the NTSA status? To check the NTSA status via USSD, send your national ID number to 22846. You'll get a status confirmation message from NTSA.

    The NTSA exam is the final hurdle before obtaining a driving license in Kenya. You can conveniently apply for the exam through the eCitizen platform. The exam is usually administered at your nearest NTSA centre or approved police station, and it evaluates theoretical knowledge and practical driving skills.
