Francis Ogolla: KDF Pilot's Family Mourns, as Kids Seek Answers on Father's Fate after Chopper Crash

  • Major George Benson Magondu did not only pilot the chief of defence forces Francis Ogolla but also senior government officials
  • His family eulogised him as a selfless and humble man whose generosity extended to the entire neighbourhood
  • He began his studies at Moi Forces Academy before advancing his training in Lanet, South Africa, Moi Airbase, and Jordan in the United States

Nancy Odindo, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics, news, and feature stories across digital and print media in Kenya.

The tragic crash of the KDF helicopter, which claimed the lives of Chief of Defence Forces General Francis Ogolla and nine other KDF soldiers, has left the family of Major George Benson Magondu devastated.

Magondu, a 29-year-old pilot, was one of the casualties in the Chesugon crash in Elgeyo-Marakwet on April 18.

Described by his family as a humble and selfless man, Magondu was deeply loved not only by his family but also by his entire community.

How Magondu's family learnt of his demise

His mother, Mary Wambui, recounted how they learned of the crash through Magondu's wife, who is also a captain in the military.

They were informed that he had sustained a severe facial injury and was rushed to the hospital, where he was later pronounced dead.

According to Citizen TV, Wambui stated that her son has left a wide gap.

"The gap is big because George was a family man. At 29, he acted like a 40-year-old father. He loved the family, the kids, and his spouse. He could sacrifice all his time and money to keep the children well, said Wambui.

"We could not sleep. Everybody was crying including little children because George was very humble," recalled a neighbour.

Magondu's children, grappling with the loss of their father, have been asking for him since the tragic incident.

"The children have been consistently asking their mother the whereabouts of their father. When my daughter hears the questions, she becomes overcome with tears not knowing how to respond," said his mother-in-law, Maureen Akinyi.

How did Magondu rise to become a major?

Magondu's aspirations were high; he not only piloted the CDF but also transported senior government officials.

His family speaks of his ambition to rise to the position of Chief of Defence Forces, a goal he pursued diligently.

Excelling academically from his time at Moi Forces Academy through his advanced training in Lanet, South Africa, Moi Airbase, and Jordan in the United States, Magondu demonstrated a commitment to his profession.

Despite his young age, he had already climbed the ranks within the KDF, attaining the rank of major.

"At 29, he had become a major. He used to say he would become the chief of defence forces," Wambui added.

He will be laid to rest at his Karimeno home in Laikipia county.

Other soldiers who died in the chopper crash

While announcing the demise of the 10 KDF soldiers, President William Ruto mentioned that only two people survived.

Besides General Ogolla and Magondu, the other eight officers who perished include Brigadier Swaleh Saidi, Colonel Duncan Keitany, Lieutenant Colonel David Sawe, Captain Sora Mohammed, Captain Hillary Litali, Senior Sergeant John Kinyua Mureithi, Sergeant Cliffonce Omondi, and Sergeant Rose Nyawira.

As of now, only three have been buried as families of two Kirinyaga soldiers plan to bury their kind on Friday, April 26.
