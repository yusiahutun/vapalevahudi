Broadcasting career

Kay started his professional career in 1982 with the New York Post as a general assignment writer. He had college-specific assignments to:

  • College Basketball
  • The National Basketball Association 
  • The New Jersey Nets

In 1989, he left the assignment coverage for the Daily News, primarily covering the New York Yankees. Kay also served as the Yankee Reporter for the Madison Square Garden Network. Between 1992 and 1999, he was the MSG’s locker room reporter for the New York Knicks.

He had previously worked for the network as a contributor on MSG SportsDesk, the news-format sports show. In 1992, he briefly left Daily News to host a sports talk show on WABC. He also returned to writing Kay’s Korner for the Daily News.

Career as an announcer

From 1992 to 2001, Michael worked with John Sterling as a radio presenter on WABC. In 1998, the duo partnered to host a WABC nightly radio show called Sports Talk with John Sterling and Michael Kay.

Jay and John hosted Yankee Talk, a weekend pre-game radio show during the baseball season, but between 1992 and 1993, he hosted his show. During the game's off-season, he continued working as a sports reporter on ABC Radio.

In 2002, he was transferred to the debuting Yes Network when WCBS acquired radio rights to Yankees broadcasts. Since then, he has remained the network's lead television play-by-play announcer. He calls around 125 games yearly for the Yes Network and Prime Video.
