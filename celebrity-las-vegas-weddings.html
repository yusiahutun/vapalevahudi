Celebrities Who Got Married in Las Vegas Weddings

2022: Jennifer Lopez and Ben Affleck

JENNIFER LOPEZ/ ON THE JLO

Two decades after first dating, Bennifer tied the knot in an intimate ceremony at the Little White Wedding Chapel. "We did it. Love is beautiful. Love is kind. And it turns out love is patient. Twenty years patient," J.Lo wrote On The JLo.

1967: Elvis and Priscilla Presley

Bettmann//Getty Images

We can't talk about Vegas weddings without noting the couple that made it cool in the first place. The King and his queen tied the knot at the Aladdin Hotel (now known as Planet Hollywood). Priscilla's younger sister, Michelle Beaulieu, served as her maid of honor.

Bettmann//Getty Images

Following their eight-minute ceremony, the pair cut into their $22,000 cake!

2019: Joe Jonas and Sophie Turner

1966: Frank Sinatra and Mia Farrow

Bettmann//Getty Images

Ol' Blue Eyes headed to Vegas for his third marriage, where he wed actress Mia Farrow at the Sands Hotel.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Hulton Archive//Getty Images

Farrow's wedding look remains one of the most talked-about in history. She stood out with this two-piece outfit, complete with a double-breasted jacket, midi skirt, and pixie haircut.

1996: Kelly Ripa and Mark Consuelos

Instagram/@kellyripa

The talk show host and her actor hubby eloped in Vegas over two decades ago. "Happy 25th Anniversary to the love of my life @instasuelos in the blink of an eye," Ripa wrote on Instagram in 2021, "because when you know, you know."

1963: Betty White and Allen Ludden

Bettmann//Getty Images

The Sands Hotel was a popular wedding spot for the biggest names of the 1960s. The actress's last word before her December 2021 passing was reportedly "Allen"—a touching reminder of how much love these two shared for so many years.

1965: Judy Garland and Mark Herron

Bettmann//Getty Images

Garland looks elegant in an embroidered lace number during her marriage to Herron. They exchanged vows in a small chapel at 1:30 a.m., surrounded only by close friends.

Bettmann//Getty Images

Here, the couple is seen signing their marriage license. After the wedding, they partied at the Sahara Hotel.

2022: Kourtney Kardashian and Travis Barker

Instagram/@kourtneykardash

At 2 a.m., these famous lovebirds "ventured out to the only open chapel with an Elvis and got married (with no license)," Kardashian wrote on Instagram. They tied the knot for real in Italy not long after, but it still counts!

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

1963: Tony Curtis and Christine Kaufmann

Bettmann//Getty Images

A year after meeting while on set of Taras Bulba in Argentina, the performers were married at the Riviera Hotel.

1958: Sammy Davis Jr. and Loray White

Bettmann//Getty Images

The Rat Pack member and his bride are surrounded by fellow stars comic Joe E. Lewis (left), singer Harry Belafonte (second from left), and actor Donald O'Connor, who served as best man. After their ceremony at the Sands Hotel, they were snapped cutting into their cake.

2002: Sarah Michelle Gellar and Freddie Prinze Jr.

Instagram/@sarahmgellar

"I love you not only for what you are, but for what I am when I am with you," Gellar shared on their 13th anniversary back in 2015. The I Know What You Did Last Summer co-stars tied the knot at the Little White Wedding Chapel and later had a ceremony in Mexico (pictured).

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

1958: Paul Newman and Joanne Woodward

Bettmann//Getty Images

The "It" couple of the late 1950s got hitched at the El Rancho Vegas Hotel. They were married until the actor's death in 2008.

1959: Elizabeth Taylor and Eddie Fisher

Bettmann//Getty Images

The film icons appear over the moon after their wedding at Temple Beth Shalom in Las Vegas. Fisher was Taylor's fourth husband; he was previously married to Taylor's friend, actress Debbie Reynolds.

2000: Marc Anthony and Dayanara Torres

Getty Images//Getty Images

Wearing matching black outfits, the singer and former Miss Universe wed in a midnight ceremony in Las Vegas—just hours after attending the Blockbuster Entertainment Awards in Los Angeles.

1975: Zsa Zsa Gabor and Jack Ryan

Keystone//Getty Images

For her sixth marriage, the actress hit Sin City with Mattel designer Jack Ryan. Fun fact: Ryan was the mind behind Barbie, Hot Wheels, and Chatty Cathy!

1955: Joan Crawford and Alfred Steele

Bettmann//Getty Images

The actress feeds her fourth and final husband, Pepsi CEO Alfred Steele, some cake following their nuptials at the Flamingo Hotel.
