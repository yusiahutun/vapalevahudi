Martina Trevisan Bio, Age, Height, Husband, Net Worth

Martina Trevisan is an Italian national who is a professional tennis player and has reached a career-high ranking of world no. 59 by the Women’s Tennis Association. She is the recipient of the WTA Tour 2022 singles title at Rabat and has also reached the semifinal at the French Open. In her entire tennis career, she has won ten singles, and two doubles titles on the ITF Women’s Circuit. While playing on the junior circuit, she possesses a win-loss record of 6-2 including 2-2 in singles and 4-0 in doubles as of March 2022.

Early Life

Martina Trevisan, 29 years old, belongs to a family of sports background where her father named Claudio Trevisan was a professional football player of his time and her brother named Matteo Trevisan was a professional tennis player on the ATP World Tour. She is known to be the younger sister of her only brother, Matteo Trevisan, who was born in 1989 and was ranked world number 1 as a junior player in 2007.

Martina Trevisan Career

Martina has played tennis throughout her career; she reached the semifinals of both the French Open and the Wimbledon Championships back in 2009. Later in 2020, she made her entry to the Grand Slam for the first time at the Australian Open where she disturbed the former Wimbledon finalist Eugenie Bouchard and later reached the main draw before falling to eventual champion, Sofia Kenin. However, she reached the quarterfinals while partnering with another player and playing in the doubles.

The same year, she appeared in the French Open and stood against the qualifiers like Camila Giorgi, who retired in the second set because of injury. Later, she stood against Coco Gauff whom she beat in three sets and she progressed to her first Grand Slam third round. She followed another win against the 20th seed Maria Sakkari, after losing the first set with 1-6 and edging the second set with a tie-break. Thereafter, she moved to her first Grand Slam quarterfinal and shocked the fifth seed Kiki Bertens but in the quarterfinals, she lost the game against another champion Iga Świątek.

Rise to Fame

In 2021, Martina appeared in the Australian Open and she partnered with Aleksandra Krunić in quarterfinal doubles. In 2022, Martina won the maiden title in Rabat and reached the WTA finals for the first time, as a result, she reached the top 60 players’ list of the world and ranked number 59 on 23rd May 2022. With her continuation and playing capabilities at the French Open 2022, she defeated other players including Harriet Dart, Magda Linette, Daria Saville, Aliaksandra Sasnovich, and 17th seed Leylah Fernandez, and thereby achieved the position ranking at number 30 in the world ranking list of tennis players. This made her reach the semifinal of the Grand Slam at the French Open 2022.
