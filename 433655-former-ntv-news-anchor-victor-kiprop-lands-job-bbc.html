Former NTV News Anchor Victor Kiprop Lands New Job at BBC

  • Victor Kiprop left NTV on Thursday, November 4, after a four-year stint at the station
  • The journalist recently joined British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC) as a business reporter
  • His exit was emotional as he was given a befitting farewell by his colleagues live on air

Former NTV news anchor Victor Kiprop has found a new home barely two weeks after leaving the twin towers.

Welcome to BBC

TUKO.co.ke has learnt Kiprop recently landed a new job at British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC).

According to an official email from BBC announcing his new role, the station noted Kiprop joined the media house as a business reporter.

The email also disclosed that Kiprop had been hired alongside fellow scribe Sara Adam, formerly of K24.

“I am pleased to announce that Victor Kiprop and Sara Adam are today joining the coolest team in the hub as business journalists. Sara has recently worked with the team as a Freelancer journalist and now joins us substantively. She’s previously worked as a business reporter and presenter at Kenya’s K24 TV. On his part, Victor Kiprop joins us from Kenya’s NTV, where he’s been working as a business reporter and presenter. Victor previously worked with CNBC Africa," read the mail in part.

Farewell party by NTV staff

Kiprop left NTV not long ago after working at the station for three years.

The latest development comes barely two weeks after Kiprop was given a befitting farewell by former colleagues at NTV.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, the business journalist said goodbye on-air and thanked the viewers and his colleagues for the support.

"This happens to be my last show here on NTV. And I have to say thank you. It feels like yesterday when I walked in and did my first show for the last ten months yet I have been here for three years. This has been a dream come true for me and an absolute honour. The show will continue with a new host and with the same energy," he said.

His co-host Gladys Gachanja also showered him with praises and said he was a great student as he joined the show and within a short time became a star in his own right.

His director also commended the journalist for the great job he has done since he moved from the business desk to the morning show.
