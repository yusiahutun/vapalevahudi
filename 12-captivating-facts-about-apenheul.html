Apenheul is a Unique Primate Park

Apenheul is not your typical zoo. Located in Apeldoorn, the Netherlands, it is a one-of-a-kind primate park that is dedicated to the conservation and welfare of various primate species.

Apenheul Has an Impressive Primate Collection

Apenheul is home to over 300 primates, representing more than 35 different species. From gibbons to lemurs, spider monkeys to tamarins, visitors can observe a wide variety of primates up close and personal.

The Park Offers a Unique Hands-On Experience

Unlike traditional zoos, Apenheul allows visitors to roam freely among the primates. With carefully designed enclosures and barriers, visitors can often have the unforgettable experience of having a primate climb over their shoulder or even sit on their lap.

Apenheul Focuses on Primate Conservation

Apenheul takes its role in primate conservation seriously. Through various initiatives, the park aims to raise awareness about the threats facing primates in the wild and support conservation projects around the world.

It all Began in 1971

Apenheul first opened its doors to the public in It started with just a few species of monkeys and has since grown into a world-renowned primate park.

Apenheul Promotes a Natural Habitat

The park’s enclosures are designed to mimic the natural habitat of the primates as closely as possible. This helps to create a more enriching environment for the animals and allows visitors to observe their natural behaviors.

Apenheul Participates in Breeding Programs

Apenheul actively participates in international breeding programs to help conserve endangered primate species. The park has had success in breeding several rare and endangered species, contributing to their long-term survival.

It Offers Educational Programs

Apenheul offers educational programs for schools and groups, allowing visitors to learn more about primate behavior, conservation, and the importance of biodiversity.

Apenheul is a Green Park

Apenheul is committed to sustainability. The park takes measures to minimize its ecological footprint, uses renewable energy sources, and promotes recycling and waste reduction.

Apenheul Hosts Special Events

Throughout the year, Apenheul hosts a variety of special events like Primate Power Days and Monkey Business. These events offer unique experiences and insights into the world of primates.

Apenheul Operates a Research Center

Apenheul is not just a park for visitors. It also operates a research center where scientists and researchers conduct studies on primate behavior, reproduction, and conservation.

Apenheul Relies on Support from Visitors

As a non-profit organization, Apenheul relies on the support and donations from visitors to continue its important work in primate conservation and welfare.

FAQs

1. How many monkey species can be found in Apenheul?

Apenheul is home to more than 30 different species of monkeys from around the world, making it one of the most diverse primate parks in the world.

2. Can visitors interact with the monkeys?

While visitors cannot directly interact with the monkeys, Apenheul offers unique opportunities for up-close encounters. You can walk through the park and observe the monkeys in their natural habitat, with many of them often coming close to the visitors.

3. Is Apenheul committed to conservation?

Absolutely! Apenheul is dedicated to the conservation of monkeys and their natural habitats. They actively participate in breeding programs, research projects, and collaborate with various organizations to protect endangered species.

4. Are there educational programs available?

Yes, Apenheul offers a range of educational programs and activities for all ages. From guided tours and workshops to interactive exhibits, visitors can learn about monkey behavior, conservation efforts, and the importance of biodiversity.

5. Can I bring my own food to Apenheul?

Yes, you are allowed to bring your own food and enjoy a picnic in designated areas within the park. However, there are also cafés and restaurants available on-site serving a variety of food and beverages.
