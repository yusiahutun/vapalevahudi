Who are Elisabeth Fritzl's siblings, and where are they today?

The saddening Fritzl case emerged in 2008. That’s after a woman named Elizabeth Fritzl told the police how she had been held captive in a makeshift cellar of their home for 24 years by her biological father. Surprisingly, the rest of Elisabeth's family members, i.e. her mother and siblings, didn't know about her captivity in their home's basement. So, who are Elizabeth Fritzl's siblings, and where are they today?

In 1984 Josef Fritzl called his daughter into the family's home basement. He was fixing a door to the renovated cellar and needed some help. As soon as it was in place, he forced Elisabeth inside after making her unconscious with an ether-soaked towel.

Over the next 24 years, Elisabeth was repeatedly tortured by her dad Josef Fritzl and gave birth to seven children. One died shortly after birth, three remained with her in the basement, and the other three lived with the family upstairs.
