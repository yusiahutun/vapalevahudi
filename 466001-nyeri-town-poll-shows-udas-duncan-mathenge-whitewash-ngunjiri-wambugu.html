Nyeri Town: New Poll Shows UDA's Duncan Mathenge Will Whitewash Ngunjiri Wambugu

  • The survey by Mizani Africa released on Wednesday, August 3, indicated that Mathenge enjoyed 56.4% support in the constituency
  • Incumbent MP Ngunjiri Wambugu of the Jubilee Party was ranked a distant second with 30.2% popularity
  • Peter Maina Mathenge and Esther Wangari Kamau were ranked third and fourth with 5.8% and 5% popularity, respectively

A new poll has shown that United Democratic Alliance (UDA) candidate Duncan Mathenge is the most preferred member of parliament contestant in Nyeri Town.

The survey by Mizani Africa released on Wednesday, August 3, indicated that Mathenge enjoyed 56.4% support in the constituency.

Incumbent MP Ngunjiri Wambugu of the Jubilee Party was ranked a distant second with 30.2% popularity.

Peter Maina Mathenge and Esther Wangari Kamau were ranked third and fourth with 5.8% and 5% popularity, respectively.

Wambugu was elected as MP for the first time in 2017 on a Jubilee Party ticket.

Ngunjiri Wambugu warns Mt Kenya residents against electing Ruto

The vocal lawmaker is a staunch supporter of Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Coalition Party presidential candidate Raila Odinga and a fierce critic of Deputy President William Ruto, who's running for the presidency on the UDA ticket.

According to opinion polls, Ruto is the most popular presidential candidate in the vote-rich Mt Kenya region, with a popularity of over 70%.

Wambugu has, on several occasions through his social media pages, warned Mt Kenya residents against voting for the Kenya Kwanza Alliance leader.

According to Wambugu, the residents will not have a say in Ruto's government should they elect him.

"Huyo kijana ni very sharp (Ruto) na kwa hivyo hawezi kupangwa. (Ruto is a very sharp person, so you cannot control him," Wambugu said in May.

Raila leads presidential race

In other news, a survey conducted by TIFA established that Raila enjoys national support of 53% against Ruto's 45%.

Notably, the research company said the results were derived from the respondents who said they intend to vote.

If the undecided respondents were included, the figures will change to 49% for Raila and 41% for Ruto.

Roots Party presidential candidate George Wajackoyah was ranked a distant third with 2% popularity, while his Agano party's counterpart Waihiga Mwaure is only supported by 0.2% of the electorate.
