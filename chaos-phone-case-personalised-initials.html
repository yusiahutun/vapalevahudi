Chaos personalised phone cases The accessory all over Instagram

Getty

If your Instagram feed is anything like ours, you won't have failed to notice that this season, it's not a J.W Anderson 'Pierce' bag or a Celine glove shoe that every member of the fashion pack has on their wish list, but a relatively inexpensive phone case. From Victoria Beckham to Kendall Jenner and Cara Delevingne, everyone worth their fashion salt is walking around with a monogrammed Chaos phone cover – and it's not simply because they are stylish.

The last few seasons has seen the 'It' bag be slowly replaced by the 'It' phone case – and it's not difficult to work out why when you consider how reliant we have become on them. Charlotte Stockdale – one of the co-founders of Chaos – explained why it made sense for them to put phone cases at the forefront of their business.

"The phone is in your hand and in your field of vision all day long," she told The Guardian. "It isn't going anywhere so we might as well make it attractive. The idea is that you put your phone on the table at a restaurant, because you've got a call you actually can't miss, and instead of people thinking you're rude they think: 'Oh, what a nice phone case.'"

[instagram ]https://www.instagram.com/p/BPiE3aGhRhc/?taken-by=chaos&hl=en[/instagram]

The accessory may have first been given a high-fashion makeover years ago – who can forget Moschino's fries? – but its presence is certainly being felt now more than ever before. Last season, Louis Vuitton made headlines by turning its iconic Petite Malle bag into a high-tech accessory and making it a centrepiece of its spring/summer 2017 collection. The £745 leather case is surely the most luxurious and desirable way to keep your phone protected this spring.

But there's a reason the Chaos case, not the Louis Vuitton one, has taken over your Instagram feed (and it's not just because of the price). When a celebrity is photographed beside a bold stamp of their initials, it is a way of imprinting their status, owning the photo and in a subtle way, promoting their personal brands.

[instagram ]https://www.instagram.com/p/BReZcLZgzT8/?taken-by=chaos&hl=en[/instagram]

This trend comes as no surprise when you consider how popular monogramming has become. From Burberry's Scarf Bar to Topshop's personalised denim service, everyone, everywhere wants to make their clothing unique. And when it comes to your phone – the one item that is almost never out of your sight – it makes sense more than anywhere else to push your personal brand.

The Chaos personalised phone cases start at £165. See shop.chaos.club for more information.
