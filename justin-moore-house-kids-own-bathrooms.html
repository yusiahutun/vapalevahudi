Justin Moore Thankful His Daughters Each Have Their Own Bathrooms

Justin Moore's three young daughters may not appreciate it right now, but down the line, they will thank their daddy for the gift of their own bathrooms.

"(The house) has enough bathrooms to where my three daughters, when they are teenagers, will not be fighting over a bathroom," Moore shares with a chuckle during an interview with Taste of Country Nights' Sam Alex. "I don’t know if that’s good or not, but it's going to help me out."

Moore and his wife, Kate, have four kids in total: Ella Kole, 8, Kennedy Faye, 6, Rebecca Klein, 4, and 1-year-old Thomas South. The family will celebrate their second year in their newly-built Arkansas home this Christmas season, but Moore admits that they still have changes they would like to make to improve their dream home.

"I don’t think its ever 100 percent finished, because we have stuff that we want to do or change or whatever," he says. "It’s bigger than I wanted it to be. You can build a lot cheaper in Arkansas than you can in Nashville."

Tweaking their dream house is just one of many priorities for the always-busy couple, whose kids are involved in various activities while Moore works his day job as a country star. Kate also recently opened a local children's clothing store called This Little Piggy.

“She's had this idea for a couple of years, and it just came to fruition," Moore explains. "We are obviously well versed in the clothing and all that, for having four kids."

Moore recently released a thought-provoking new single in "The Ones That Didn’t Make It Back Home" and plans release a new album early next year. He'll also hit the road on a tour in support of new music.
