Designer Anissa Kermiche Doesn't Care About Looking Effortless

Anissa Kermiche's calling as a jewelry and homewares designer began with a rather bold career pivot. Before she founded her eponymous brand, she was an engineer working at a prestigious consulting firm in France. "I was helping airports and supermarkets organize their supply chains, doing all kinds of equations," she laughs, considering how much her day-to-day has changed since then.

Christian Louboutin

At 27, she used her holiday leave to attend a summer course at a prestigious London design school. When it ended, she flew home to Paris, quit her job, and relocated to the UK to study 3D design full time, supporting herself by teaching French and selling jewelry on the side. "I made a few jewelry pieces while I was studying, and whenever I was wearing them, people would ask where I bought them. So I made a few pieces for friends—it was a 'fake it till you make it' kind of situation."

And made it she has. Eleven years on, Kermiche is a household name in the fashion world, and we'd be shocked if you haven't seen her eclectic jewelry on some of the world's most stylish women, or her famous "butt vases" adorning their shelves.

Kermiche's stunning London showroom recently hosted a celebration of the launch of Loubi54, Christian Louboutin's popular evening clutch, which has recently been reimagined in a crossbody style in a slate of stunning colors. It's a fitting pairing, as glamorous accents like the Loubi54 crossbody are exactly the kind of pieces you'll find working overtime in Kermiche's own wardrobe.

Christian Louboutin

"My clothing is very minimal—I love jeans, a well-cut T-shirt, a blazer," she says. "So I rely a lot on accessories to bring the personality. They're like the finishing touch, the lacquer. I like quite daring, colorful bags—they steal the show. You can show up with a simple black dress and an almost bare face, carrying a beautiful vivid bag, and it just makes the whole thing look so stylish and well thought through."

"I love the size of the Loubi54—it's a bag that can follow you through your life."

Christian Louboutin

That said, Kermiche rarely sacrifices function for form. "I don't understand this trend of the mini-mini bags, because it's just not comfortable. And what's not comfortable is not empowering," she explains. "I love the size of the Loubi54—it's a bag that can follow you through your life. When you can keep a little book, a mirror, a bit of makeup in your bag, then it becomes your best companion."

Christian Louboutin

Combining sophistication and practicality, the Loubi54 line pays homage to the iconic New York nightclub era of the 1970s, as well as its Parisian counterparts where Christian Louboutin used to go with his friends. Kermiche was unsurprised to learn that the iconic French designer often draws from this period for inspiration. "It's an era that has had a really strong influence on how women in France dress to this day," she says.

Christian Louboutin

Paris-born Kermiche's personal style is a glamorous blend of influences from her hometown and London, the city she now calls home. She's certainly got a refreshing honesty on what it really takes to look so chic. "I don't really care about looking effortless," she says with charming sincerity. "This whole obsession about looking like you didn't try—I think it's quite rude, actually. I like people to think I made an effort for them and for myself."

Whether it's through her hair, her handbag, or her perfectly lacquered nails—"not a very French thing to do, by the way"—having feminine, fun details are important to her. "Even if it's something like a gemstone on the nail, I'm not afraid of doing something that might be seen as tacky or high-maintenance."

"I'm not saying every woman has to be sexy—but if she wants to, no one should find that less chic."

If you've seen Kermiche's designs, you'll know she loves to pay homage to the female form in all its natural glory. It's a philosophy that carries over into her wardrobe. "I prefer to celebrate femininity and all its splendor and shape," she says. "I'm not saying every woman has to be sexy or sexualized—but if she wants to, no one should find that less chic."

Christian Louboutin

It comes as no surprise, then, that Kermiche has plenty of pieces from Christian Louboutin—long lauded as one of the sexiest designer brands out there. "The classic point pumps are really sexy," she says. "I remember when they first came out, I would wear them with a mini and bare legs. They really allowed women to express their sexiness." These days, she's more partial to shoes that keep her a bit closer to the ground. "I love a Mary Jane—I have an extended collection of them, including the Louboutin Jane shoe. I think they suit pretty much every woman and go with anything: jeans, miniskirts, dresses."

Christian Louboutin

That's because for Kermiche, comfort is paramount. Quality over quantity ("I don't follow trends") and dressing for one's body are her other pillars of personal style: "Not everything suits everyone. I think it's really important to understand your body shape and move toward pieces that make you feel good, instead of looking at what other people are wearing. Because by being comfortable, you'll go more places, do more things, and feel more empowered."
