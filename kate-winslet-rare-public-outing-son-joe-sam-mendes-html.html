Despite being a Hollywood A-lister, Winslet has done her best to keep all three of her children out of the spotlight.

She's also spoken openly about being a hands-on mom and how she manages co-parenting three children with three different fathers.

She previously revealed to Vogue that she has full custody of her kids and that they 'don't go back and forth' between homes.

'[My kids have] always been with me,' she told the outlet in 2013. 'They don't go from pillar to post; they're not flown here and there with nannies. That's never happened.'

She continued: 'My kids don't go back and forth; none of this 50/50 time with the mums and dads – my children live with me; that is it. That is it!'

Winslet's remarks sparked mild backlash as a fathers' rights group accused her of being 'against shared parenting.'

But Mendes was quick to shutdown the chatter by insisting that his rights as a father have never been infringed upon.

In the same interview, Winslet said she refuses to hire housekeepers, personal chefs and other help because it's not something she wants her children 'exposed to.'

'I remember my mother cooking and that's being a parent, and that's what I want to do,' she said.

Shortly after finalizing their divorce, Winslet opened up to Harper's Bazaar in 2011 about co-parenting with Mendes and how their child's happiness comes first.
