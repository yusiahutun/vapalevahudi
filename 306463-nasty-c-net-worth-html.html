Sources of wealth

Here is a look at the different ways the accomplished rapper has made his wealth.

Music career

The rapper joined the music industry at a relatively young age. At the age of 15, he released his debut mixtape titled One Kid, a Thousand Coffins. He followed this up with his debut EP titled LAME (Levitating Above My Enemies) in 2014. He released his second mixtape that included the hugely popular track Juice Back one year later.

Endorsements, commercials, and sponsorships

Nasty C's massive appeal among his fans has made him a good choice for brand ambassador roles for different companies. Here is a look at the endorsement deals the rapper has landed over the years.

Nasty C has appeared in several commercials for Puma in South Africa. He is among the few South African hip-hop artists to land a deal with a major clothing company. Nasty C's deal with Puma saw him premier the then-new PUMA Evolution Hi Blocked Sneakers.

Mercedes Benz

Nasty C's deal with Mercedes is quite a lucrative one. The young rapper signed a deal with the German carmaker to participate in the 'Grow up Your Own Way' campaign. This was the carmaker's attempt to take on a new marketing approach.

In 2018, Nasty C began collaborating with the local fashion brand Redbat. The deal allows the rapper to have his fashion label distributed through the brand's Sportscene stores across the country. The range of clothes included in the deal included sweaters, denim jackets, and t-shirts.

In 2019, the rapper became the face of Axe in South Africa in what is still one of his biggest endorsement deals to date. The company aimed to leverage the young rapper's appeal among the youth to empower and show commitment to South Africa's young generation. The deal involved Axe's campaign dubbed 'Find Your Magic.'
