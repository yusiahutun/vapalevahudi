Ariana Grande and Dalton Gomez's 'separation date' revealed as they file for divorce

Ariana Grande and Dalton Gomez have reportedly simultaneously filed for divorce, three months after it was revealed the two had gone their separate ways. Her rumored boo, Ethan Slater also filed for divorce from his High School sweetheart, Lilly Jay in July.

The music star tied the knot with her husband Dalton Gomez in May 2021. However, since spending time in the UK filming Wicked, the two went their separate ways. She’s now rumored to be with her co-star, although neither has commented on this.

Ariana Grande and Dalton Gomez ‘file for divorce’ 7 months after separation

After she was spotted without her ring at Wimbledon, this July, rumors began to fly that there was trouble in paradise between the two. Just days later, it was reported the two had officially separated, and they’ve now allegedly filed for divorce.

It is said the Thank U, Next singer and her ex stated  “irreconcilable differences” as the reason for divorce, in documents obtained by TMZ. The publication states that Gomez “immediately followed suit.”

The publication also claims that the two had a prenup in place, with the date of separation marked all the way back to February 2023, seven months ago.

Sources tell the publication that it took so long to file as “the two took their time to iron out details and settle.”

Insiders also tell TMZ that there are no hard feelings between the former couple. “They’ve been really caring and respectful of one another every step of this process.”

At the time news of separation broke, sources claimed that post-pandemic, “their differences became alarmingly apparent.” The LA realtor is said to have been “taken aback” by Ariana’s celebrity status, including paps and demands for her to be away.

GRV Media and Celebrity Tidbit have contacted representatives of Ariana Grande for comment.

The star’s new tattoo ‘linked to new love’

Ethan and Ariana are yet to comment on their rumored new love, however, it is thought her new tattoo may be linked to the love story.

In a recent interview with Allure, the 30-year-old opened up about her new ink, and the meaning behind it.

“It’s Glinda from the original L. Frank Baum book. I waited forever to fill up this hand. And I thought, ‘I’m gonna wait for the right thing to come along,’ and yeah, that felt very right,” she revealed.

The SpongeBob SquarePants musical star is playing Boq alongside Grande, who plays Glinda, in the upcoming Wicked film.

The film is expected to release in theatres at the end of 2024.
