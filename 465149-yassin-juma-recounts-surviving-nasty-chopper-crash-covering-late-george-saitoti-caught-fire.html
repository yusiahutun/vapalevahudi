Yassin Juma Recounts Surviving Nasty Chopper Crash While Covering Late George Saitoti: &quot;It Caught Fi

  • Yassin Juma has given a chilling account of how he survived a helicopter crash in Kapsabet 13 years ago
  • Yassin had accompanied the then Interior Security minister George Saitoti, his assistant Orwa Ojode and other officials on a tour of the region
  • The plane developed a mechanical problem at 12pm and crashed at the Kipchoge Keino stadium, injuring some of its occupants but no one lost their life

Journalist Yassin Juma has given a hair-raising account of how he survived a horrific plane crash while on assignment to cover the late Prof. George Saitoti, the then Interior Security minister.

Plane crash occurred at 12 noon

Yassin was among the six cameramen from local media houses who accompanied the men in charge of the security docket for a pertinent tour of the Rift Valley region.

According to a report by Citizen TV at the time, the accident occurred around 12 noon at the Kipchoge Keino stadium in Kapsabet.

The plane was just taking off when it developed a mechanical problem and crashed with 26 passengers on board.

Among the people on board were the then Police Commissioner Major Retired Hussein Ali, then Assistant Minister for Internal Security Orwa Ojode, Rift Valley Provincial Commissioner Hassan Noor Hassan and other senior police officers.

Yassin survived chopper crash, two motor accidents

"I don't know how I survived this helicopter crash in 2009 God has been good to me throughout my career one chopper crash...two nasty motor accidents in wartorn Mogadishu and the remote Karamoja Uganda," said Yassin. "I was the last person to come out of the chopper and a few minutes later it caught fire, " he added.

In an interview with TUKO.co.ke, Yassin said he was the last person to come out of the plane because he hit his head on the door and was unable to undo his seat belt.

"I was opposite my cameraman and was knocked out for about five to ten minutes. When I regained consciousness i saw people pulling me but I leg got caught up in the seat belt," he said."The whole village was screaming and my resceuers finally managed to get me out and shortly after the plane caught fire," he explained.

Yassin revealed that the then Police Commissioner, a trained paratrooper, was the only one who dramatically escaped mid-air...when it crashed down near Kapsabet Primary School.

Passengers walked over him to safety

"I hit my head on the old soviet craft and fell unconscious; 18 passengers walked over me to save their lives...I couldnt escape bound by the seat belt ( I never buckle up on planes ever since this crash)," he narrated.

"I woke up to Kalenjin speaking men rescuing me...my head bleeding from a cut I was like ala Kikalee ndio language inabongwa heaven (Is Kalenjin the official language in heaven? I thought I was dead. Ojode survived this crash...but not the second on in which Saitoti too perished...RIP," he said.

Yassin now bears a scar on the back of his head to remind him of this lucky escape.
