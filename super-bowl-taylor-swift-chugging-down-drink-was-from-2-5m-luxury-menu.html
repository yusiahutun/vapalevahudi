Super Bowl: Taylor Swift chugging down drink was from '$2.5m luxury menu'

Taylor Swift joined Travis Kelce’s family in one of the Allegiant Stadium suites in Las Vegas for the Super Bowl, and had quite a lot of choice when it comes to the impressive food menu.

Taylor Swift was in attendance at this year’s Super Bowl to support her NBA-playing beau. Travis Kelce was playing alongside his Kansas City Chief’s teammates against the San Francisco 49ers. The Chiefs won 25-22 after playing overtime, but while sitting waiting (anxiously, we assume) Tay Tay had quite a luxe choice of snacks and meals to eat. Although she was ‘booed’ by the crowd when she downed her drink, fans defended the star for enjoying herself.

Super Bowl’s incredible menu inside Taylor Swift’s suite

According to Page Six, the food menu Taylor Swift could have chosen from at the Super Bowl cost a whopping $2.5 million to host. The Las Vegas Raiders and Levy Restaurants partnered with the stadium to deliver the list of delicacies available and it was made in-house by the Silver & Black Hospitality Group.

Items that were up for purchase by the celebrities included: lobster, breakfast for dinner, which included buttermilk fried chicken with waffles and maple syrup, Sin City’s king crab fried rice, Wagyu tenderloin, and Vegas “greatest” shrimp cocktail tower.

Other food that could be purchased included bacon-wrapped hot dogs, barbecue “burnt end” burritos, carne asada fries, seafood stuffed potatoes, nachos, Wagyu beef hotdogs.

Deserts up for grabs were the Super Bowl chocolate box, Glazed Donut Super Bowl Sundae, frozen cheesecake, and the Hot Dog Macaron: a chocolate-covered banana and macaron with vanilla ganache, and strawberry sauce.

A souvenir popcorn tub could also be brought on the door and there was also a service bar, according to this TikTok.

The drinks’ menu which the music star was able to choose from included some specially-made drinks. They included The Rumming Back (with Captain Morgan, elderflower liqueur, golden passion mix, and a chilled ginger beer), First and Tequila (with Casamigos Blanco, Cointreau, lime juice, and fresh lemon sour), and  the Sin City Sour (with Marie Brizard apricot liqueur, fresh lemon sour, and ginger beer).

Fans gush over the menu

Taylor Swift wasn’t the only famous face in the building to enjoy the food menu at this year’s Super Bowl. Celebrity couple Ryan Reynolds and Blake Lively joined Tay Tay in her box. Beyoncé and her family were also there as she announced the release of her next album and surprise-dropped two new singles in a carefully-placed commercial.

In the comments, people couldn’t believe the cost of it all and had some pretty heavy criticism about it.

One person penned: “For that price, there should be chefs making it fresh as you order. Not having all of the food sitting there for hours.”

A second commented: “I could pay a private chef to do it and keep like 2.4 mill and watch it on TV fr.”

Another said: “That’s it????”

Someone else wrote: “The buyer should be able to customize their food menu two weeks in advance, Hotfogs and fries is not a flex at this price point!”

A fifth joked: “For 2.5 million I better keep a player.”
