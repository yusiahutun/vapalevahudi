The many faces of Kate Moss:

She's one of the most iconic supermodels in the world, having burst onto the scene as a fresh-faced 14-year-old. 

Coming from humble beginnings in Croydon, the then teen was scouted by Sarah Doukas, the owner of modelling agency Storm, at JFK Airport in New York following a holiday in The Bahamas. 

And while the talent scout believed in her looks, neither Kate nor her mother had the confidence that her appearance would propel her to the big time with Kate stating in a 2022 interview that she didn't think she was 'modelling material'.

While her mother Linda stated that she 'wasn't photogenic', with Kate agreeing before both then concluding that she 'might as well try' to make it in the modelling industry.

She soon rose to prominence in the early nineties thanks to her images by Corinne Day featured in youth style magazine The Face, with her radiant features and unstyled shots going on to pique the interest of those in the industry.

Jump forward a few years and she's one of the most in demand models on the planet, commanding the catwalk with the likes of Naomi Campbell and Claudia Schiffer despite being dubbed the 'anti-supermodel' thanks to her 5 foot seven inch frame and more natural look. 

Over the years Kate's career has always been smooth sailing, with the star having weathered the 'Cocaine Kate' storm of 2005 after infamously being pictured next to lines of white powder.

Her years of hard partying, cigarettes and alcohol inevitably took its toll.

However, after overhauling her lifestyle and embarking on a new direction of clean living and healthy eating, Kate appears more youthful than ever and still turns heads on the runway thanks to her smooth complexion and glowing skin. 

With Kate, who's set to turn 50 next year, still dazzling in the fashion industry, MailOnline takes a look at her changing faces over the years...

1985-1993 

Born in 1974, Kate was just an everyday school girl before a model scout changed her world forever.

Back in a 2018 interview with Love Magazine, Kate reflected on her looks when growing up, admitting that she'd  'never, never, never' contemplated a career as a model before being spotted.

2000 - 2001

By the 2000s, Kate was in her mid twenties and still looking as stunning as ever.

2003-2006

Kate was notorious for her partying in the late nineties and early noughties.

However, she was hit with a drug scandal in 2005 when she was pictured allegedly taking cocaine at a party.

Kate wasn't charged with any offence and later told how it caused a lot of upheaval in her life after she lost contracts with a number of top fashion labels. 

Her hedonistic lifestyle and the late nights began to take its toll.

2006- 2010

After she was cleared of any drugs charges, Kate returned to modelling and signed 18 contracts for the autumn-winter 2006 season.

She worked for the likes of Rimmel, Agent Provocateur, Calvin Klein and Burberry among others.

In 2007 she went for a new hairstyle, getting her blonde looks cut into a fringe, and her glowing looks returned.

In 2016, Kate founded the Kate Moss Agency (KMA), with her clients including singer Rita Ora, actress Esmé Creed-Miles and model Jordan Barrett among others.

Meanwhile, Kate still continued to model herself, revealing in 2017 she favoured a secret beauty trick similar to her famous Croydon facelift.

'A great trick they do on photoshoots is doing these little braids underneath your hair to pull your skin up, like a little lift,' she said.

2020-2023 

Last year, Kate revealed her secret beauty weapon, admitting she carries a £25 jade roller in her handbag.

'I love it for under my eyes; it irons them out a bit,' she told Elle magazine. 'I keep one in my bag—when I’m bored, I just start rolling!'

Last year Kate sparked speculation she'd had a facelift but the star never confirmed if she went under the knife.

Harley Street cosmetic surgeon Dr Graziela Crisovan told heat: 'Kate’s face does look slightly fuller, which may be a result of a liquid face lift with fillers. The fine lines around her eyes are reduced, which may be a result of anti-wrinkle injections, and her skin has a lovely glowing complexion, which could be achieved with injectable moisturisers like Profhilo.'

And last year she was signed up as the creative director of Diet Coke to work on a special collection and advertising campaign films around the brand's 40th anniversary.

Kate said she was 'thrilled to join the Diet Coke family', adding: 'I love the past collaborations they’ve done with such incredible names in fashion.'
