Behind the scenes at the Bordello: The world of the modern courtesan revealed in stunning photographs

  • The incredible photos are the work of Australian artist, Vee Speers
  • All were shot in original 1920s and 30s Parisian bordellos
  • Images are to go on show in the UK for the first time at a London gallery
  • Fans of Speers' work include Chanel creative director, Karl Lagerfeld

By Ruth Styles

Shot in smoky black and white and softly lit, the images in Vee Speers' Bordello series could have been taken from a book of 1920s daguerreotypes.

Antique though they might appear, the Australian photographer's evocative images were actually shot in modern day Paris and offer a contemporary peek at the world of the courtesan.

Now the photographs, which were inspired by the view from Speers' flat in Paris' Red Light district, are to go on display in the UK for very first time.

Australian photographer Vee Spears has taken a contemporary peek at the world of the courtesan inspired by the view from her flat in Paris's red-light district

Favourite: Speers' intimate portraits have proved hugely popular with creatives including Karl Lagerfeld

Among the photos to be shown at the Little Black Gallery in Chelsea is a haunting scene that shows a girl looking coquettishly over her shoulder in an oriental-themed brothel and another that shows girls in dehumanising Phantom of the Opera style masks.

Others ape the decadent world of Paris nightlife in the 1920s and 30s and were shot in the same lavishly decorated bordellos that were immortalised at the time by Hungarian photographer, Brassaï.

Haunting: The photos evoke the decadent nightlife of 1920s Paris and the demi-monde that enjoyed it

Decadent: Speers' modern courtesans were photographed in original 1920s and 30s bordellos

'From my window I can see the girls waiting downstairs in the doorways, and it has always fascinated me,' Speers revealed in an interview with industry bible, Lens Culture.

'The art of seduction fascinates me. And it gives you power. I tried to capture all that when I photographed these women, none of whom are models.'

Orient: One of the loveliest old bordellos was one that retained its original Far Eastern style decor

Inspiration: Speers says the series was inspired by her Paris home which sits in the red-light district

Speers, who has lived in Paris since 1990, is famous for her sensual portraits and boasts Chanel supremo Karl Lagerfeld among her fans.

Writing on Speers' website, the notoriously opinionated designer said: 'The twilight of Vee Speers’ great photos of beautiful women causes the viewer to merge with the surroundings of the image and enter a kind of dream-like vision of an often sordid reality.

'Shapes lose definition and imagination outweighs perception. Real life is left behind… the boundaries between subject and photographer become more and more indistinct.

'She shows beauty where beauty can be terribly absent.'

Vee Speers: Bordello opens at The Little Black Gallery in Chelsea on 4th March. See thelittleblackgallery.com for more information.
