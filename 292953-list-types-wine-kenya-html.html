List of types of wine in Kenya

Some people enjoy taking it with a nice dinner. Others crave for it after a long day. At celebrations, it always finds its way onto your table. Yes. We are talking about wine. It is the center of happiness for most people and is said to have numerous health benefits if taken in moderation. But with so many different types of wine in Kenya, which one do you choose? The answer to this question solely depends on your taste buds and personal preferences.

Wine is an alcoholic drink that is produced from fermented grapes. It is the most consumed alcohol in the whole world after beer. This is mainly because of its compatibility with most food making the meal complete. There are countries in the world that wouldn't consider a meal complete without a glass of wine. Different wines made from the same grape could have a difference in taste if the grapes were grown in different regions. Conditions such as acidity of the soil, amount of rainfall, the humidity of the region, temperatures of the region and soil additives affect the taste of grapes. This explains why we have so many brands of wine in Kenya as we import grapes from different parts of the world. The following list outlines the major types of wines in Kenya.

Red wines

The red wine varieties are the most common wines in Kenya. They are made from a variety of grapes giving them the broad red wine brands. They include;

Merlot

This is among the sweet red wines in Kenya. It is the most adorned and is stocked by many wines distributors. It is low in acidity and has a sweet, soft and fruity taste profile. It is a ready-to-drink variety and can complement with almost any type of food. Its dry smoothness makes it a popular choice, especially in restaurants. The Merlot wine is considered to be among the sweet red wines in Kenya by many. The grapes used to make this type of wines grows in many regions and can respond well to cooler climates which makes it very adaptable.

Pinot Noir

It is a medium body wine and is rarely blended with other grape varieties. It is a great choice for all types of wine drinkers. This type of wine mostly comes from France but is found in other regions like California, Austria, and New Zealand. This wine contains elements of black cherry, raisin and strawberry. It is great for dishes with creamy sauces.

Shiraz

This wine is pronounced with two words; Syrah in European and Shiraz in Californian or Australian. It is a full-bodied and hearty brand. Generally set aside for those who prefer a stronger taste and texture. It is medium dark-red. It is also a flavorful wine and had a spicy taste and a fruity scent with hints of chocolate. It is best served with complement for chilli and spicy foods. Also great to drink with beef.

Red Zinfandel

It is a red wine prepared from the popularly grown grape in California. However much of it is turned into the sweet Zinfandel white wine. The red Zinfandel is very versatile since the grapes used to make it are highly adaptable to the manipulation of the manufacturer. It is very fruity, with plum, raspberry and cherry tastes. It is one of the heaviest and strongest wines in the Kenyan market. It is not recommended for beginners

Beaujolais

This is a much lighter body than some of the common wines in Kenya, and is perfect for beginners. It has a lower alcohol content, fruity flavor and lighter texture allowing beginners to get wine taste before moving on to stronger types of wines.

Cabernet Sauvignon

This is full-bodied red wine. It is intensely flavored with a rich texture and is the darkest of red wines. It ages very well to improve its taste, usually 15-30 months in the barrels. The aging gives the wine a toasted vanilla and cedar taste. It also has elements such as plum, black berry and spice. It is considered by some to be one of the best wines in Kenya and in the world. Some of the famous Cabernet Sauvignon come from France, Chile, California, and Austria.

White wines in Kenya

White wines are the most esteemed and highly rated among the common types in Kenya. This is because they offer users usage flexibility. They are basically made from white or black wine grape. Users have an option to choose between dry white wines or the sweet and gold. The white wines have conspicuous differences and similarity, and they can all be used with a variety of food. These wines include;

Chardonnay

This wine has a light yellow color and is popular among users because of its distinct smell and taste similar to that of a fresh green apple. Identifying it by smell only is hard. This white wine is usually differentiated with other white wines by its aging and fermentation process in the oak barrels. The aging chardonnay in an oak barrel gives it a unique smell ranging from nutty to creamy. It can have different tastes like that of vanilla, pear, and lemon, pineapple, crisp, peach or even buttery. It is classified as a white table wine and best complements a meal of fish or poultry.

Sauvignon Blanc

This is another famous white wine and has its origin in France. It is one of the most aromatic with a scent of citrus and grass. The wine has a bitter aftertaste with hints of gooseberry, melon and black currant. It is definitely a good choice to complement salads. Pay attention when buying from wine distributors because some French and South African winemakers produce dry versions that taste similar to the Sauvignon Blanc.

Pinot Grigio

It is more colorful than other white wines and is ranked top of the most popular white wines in Italy. It is mildly perfumed and very soft. This white wine has flowers, subtle spices, pear and citrus elements. The acidity of this wine gives it a nice crispness. It does well when served cold accompanied by cheese.

Riesling

It is a lower alcohol dry wine and it originates from the popular German grapes. It is distinguished by a distinctive fruit and acid balance. It has high natural acidity helping it cut the sweet taste. This white wine is pale in color with sweet elements of honeysuckle and peach. Unlike other types of wines, it is rarely prepared in oak barrels. This makes it adaptable to various types of foods including hot and spicy choices and is best served with pork. Users will notice floral aromas and a fruity flavor like pear, apple, and apricot.

Gewurztraminer

This white wine is floral with moderate alcohol. It originates from Germany and comes in various zests that leave a sweet aftertaste. It tastes like grapefruit and is a perfect spicy food accompaniment. Gewürztraminer is best served during a barbeque. This wine as other flavors such as rose petal aromas, lychee and talc.

Pinot Blanc

Pinot Blanc is a semi-dry white wine, lighter and repeatedly compared to Chardonnay. It is naturally crispy and refreshing depending on it region of origin. This wine might come in a nutty flavor or a sweet taste.

Provence

Ranked top of the rose wines and is versatile. This makes them perfect for both a partner for food as well as an aperitif. They originate from southeast France in the region of Provence. This wine pairs well with a variety of foods thanks to it being fresh, crispy and dry. It is perfect for barbeques. It is usually made from an assortment of grapes which may include; Mourverde, Syrah, Cinsault and Grenache grapes. It has a pale pink color.

Grenache

Also known as Garnacha in Spain. It is low in tanning and acidity. It has lovely flavors of cherry. It has a lot of taste when served cold, and it is therefore advisable to leave it in the fridge for some time before indulging.

Tavel Rose

This type of wine is also known as the “The King of Roses”. It has a complex aroma of summer fruits with hints of spice. It can be served as an aperitif or with herb sausages, seafood pasta, cold fish starter or a variety of cheese.

Champagnes in Kenya

Champagnes are also sparkling wines. The difference between the two is the grapes used to produce them. They are produced from a region called champagne in France. They are commonly used during celebrations. There are two categories of champagne namely; vintage and non-vintage champagnes. The variance between the two is that vintage champagnes are made from one single year harvest while the latter is made from a combination of different year harvests. Champagnes are made from three types of grapes;

In conclusion, as you enjoy these different types of wines in Kenya, please remember to keep your fingers on the stem of the glass, especially for white and sparkling wines. Otherwise, the temperature from your fingers is sure to warm the wine, interfering with its characteristics. To have a good experience with wine, spin it in your glass. The oxidation will release the aroma. As you do this you will see little stripes of clear liquid running down the glass. If thin, it means that the fermentation process was well achieved and naturally done.
