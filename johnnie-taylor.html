Biography

Johnnie Taylor was born in Crawfordsville, AR on May 5, 1934. Legendary singer who sang music in a variety of genres, from doo-woop to disco to R&B and soul, over five decades of work. After signing with Stax Records in 1966, he began recording with the house band Booker T. & the MGs and their guitarist Steve Cropper. His first musical group out of school was for the gospel group Highway QC’s, founded by a young Sam Cooke, whom Taylor replaced.
