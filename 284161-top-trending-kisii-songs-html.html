Top trending Kisii songs - Tuko.co.ke

Kisii songs are part and parcel of the cultural identity of the people from the Gusii highlands. One can easily recognize the songs through the instruments used without really having to know the language. Those who know the Kisii language though have an added advantage because they do not only get entertained, but they also take the message. Kisii songs address various issues such as marriage, peace, education and religion, among many other things.

Before we delve into Kisii tracks, perhaps we ought to ask first, who are the Kisii people? They are a Bantu community that made the Gusii highlands located in the Nyanza parts of Kenya their home. Music has evolved all over the world and Kisii is no exception. You might come across people referring to vernacular tracks as traditional Kisii songs, but nothing could be further from the truth.

There are new forms of music emanating from Kisii than there were decades ago. To add to that, Kisii musicians have also adopted the use of modern equipment and instruments to make their music more appealing and marketable. In a way, they are killing two birds with one stone because they are expanding the reach of their music and at the same time preserving the cultural heritage of the people.

Ebarasa is a song that many people will resonate with the introduction being a conversation about daily challenges such as lack of school fees for children, gambling problems and neighbour disputes, thus necessitating ebarasa (baraza). Baraza in Kisii usually means an emergency meeting, especially the ones called by chiefs and village elders.

Miggy is trying to pass a message that there are things that need to be addressed, such as jealous neighbours who do not want him to progress in life. The video of the song is self-explanatory showing people gathered and a chief listening to the woes of Miggy.
