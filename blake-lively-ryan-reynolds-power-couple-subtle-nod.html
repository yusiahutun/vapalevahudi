The subtle way Blake Lively affirms she and Ryan Reynolds are the 'best duo'

The parents-of-four are balancing Wrexham, a new baby, and now gin and tonic

    Blake Lively and Ryan Reynolds just found the smartest way to affirm that they are, in fact, Hollywood's best power couple.

    In honor of National Gin and Tonic Day on April 9th, Blake's premium sparkling mixer line, Betty Buzz, revealed its own take on the Betty Buzz Gin and Tonic.

    View post on Instagram 

    Blake subtly featured Ryan in her new Betty Buzz spot

    In a video made for the campaign, Blake was seen in her kitchen making a fizzy gin and tonic, lemon wedge and all, and what better gin to include than from the brand Aviation Gin aka Ryan's line of gin.

    The promotional spot played it up, citing that the tonic and gin were the best duo since "Clyde and Bonnie. Watson and Holmes. Cheese and mac. Jelly and peanut butter," even calling it a real "Juliet and Romeo love story."

    Her appearance in the campaign also marked Blake's first true social media showing since they welcomed their fourth child during the holiday season.

    Fans caught on pretty quickly, leaving comments along the lines of: "If a drink was a power couple," and: "I see someone threw in the aviation gin #ryanreynolds. Smooth move wifey," as well as: "Gin and Tonic is as classic as 'peas n' carrots' 'ham n' cheese' 'pb n' j'… 'Blake n' Ryan'."

    The pair have kept a relatively low profile since welcoming their fourth child despite being quite active on the professional front with Ryan's Mint Mobile sale and Wrexham wins and Blake's upcoming role in the adaptation of It Ends With Us.

    The two did make their first post-birth appearance together at a recent set of Wrexham AFC games with all four of their children, daughters James, eight, Betty, six, Inez, three, and their newborn.

    The notoriously private couple made sure not to give anything away when it came to revealing their baby's gender or name, however.

    While this was most of Ryan's family's first time in Wrexham, his eight-year-old James previously joined her dad during a visit to the stadium earlier in the year when they watched Wrexham AFC take on Sheffield United.
