You would think after an Instagram related scandal involving an underage girl, James Franco would tone down his use of the social media.

But the actor has only stepped up his questionable campaign of posting near naked bed selfies.

On Saturday, the 36-year-old posted one of his raciest pictures yet.

'I was waiting for you!': James Franco posted yet another racy selfie to Instagram on Saturday

In the image the Spring Breakers star appears to be naked in bed but has a sheet draped across him to stop him revealing his nether regions.

However, the star takes it one step further suggestively leaving his hand under the bedding.

With his other arm outstretched, the star stares down his phone's camera lens.

Taking inspiration from sexting, James captioned the picture: 'WTF HAPPENED? I was waiting for you!!!!!'

'BED SELFIE WITH KEEGAN ALLEN!!!!! TIME TO GO NUTS!!!!' James Franco and Keegan Allen took their budding bromance to the next level on Tuesday as they posed together for a sexy shirtless 'bed selfie'

Earlier this month month the actor found himself in hot water after attempting to pick up an underage fan via photo sharing site Instagram.

He later apologized on the morning talk show Live With Kelly and Michael, saying he used 'bad judgment' and 'learned my lesson'.

But that lesson has not stopped the near naked selfies.

On Tuesday, he enlisted pal Keegan Allen for another 'bed selfie' and the two men snuggled up under a blanket.

He's clearly moved on from the 'embarrassment': Despite being busted earlier this month for trying to arrange a hookup with an underage fan via Instagram, that hasn't stopped the 36-year-old from his cheeky antics, regularly posting selfies from his bed in recent weeks

AP reported this week that the This Is The End star talked about his fascination taking pictures like this saying they were a 'kind of intimate portrait' of himself and to get people talking.

'It's not like I'm exposing myself or anything,' he said in the interview on Thursday.

He said when he takes pictures of himself 'it's almost like it's connected to you' and that by putting 'that intimate space out there it's kind of this new thing that we're all getting used to'.

The director wrote in a New York Times essay last December titled The Meanings Of The Selfie that he has 'become increasingly addicted to Instagram' and acknowledged that he has 'been accused of posting too many of them'.

'APPLE SLICES IN MY BED - I CAN'T BELIEVE YOU DIDNT SHOW UP!' The heartthrob shared this racy snap just last week with his almost 1.8 million followers

Meanwhile, Emma Roberts recently opened up about playing a schoolgirl who flirts with her soccer coach (played by James) in their new film Palo Alto.

'It was fun,' Emma told People about her steamy scenes with James.

'Neither of us made it awkward, so it was great. And also, I think the fact that I was not actually 17 probably made things less weird. I was 21. I think that that kind of made things feel like we’re pretending – this isn’t real.'
