The story of David Temple: Where is he now?

Cold cases are the subject of some of the most popular TV shows, movies, and documentaries globally, especially in the U.S. Most of the cold cases go unsolved for numerous decades. The David Temple documentary tells the tale of a murder that transpired back in 1999, in which Belinda Temple was murdered. The case remains unsolved 22 years down the line.

David came to the spotlight in the year 1999 when his wife was found murdered in their house. He was found guilty of the crime twice and has served ten years in prison for the crime. The David Temple murder case, although having gone through court twice, is yet to be closed completely, more than two decades later.

1992: Belinda and David Temple get married

David and Belinda got married in 1992. The two met when they were studying at Stephen F. Austin State University. After completing their university education, they were both employed as high school teachers.

Belinda was a special education teacher, whereas David was a football coach. They were blessed with a son named Evan Temple. At the time of her death, Belinda was eight months pregnant with a baby girl.

January 1999: Belinda Temple found dead in her house

On January 11, 1999, Belinda Temple received a phone call from the Daycare centre where she used to take her son, informing him that he had a fever. She immediately left work and rushed to pick him from the daycare. Given that he had a meeting at work, he called her husband in to take care of Evan that afternoon.

When she came back from work, she passed at her in-law's house to pick soup for Evan, and that was the last time she was seen alive. When David came home, he noticed that the back door glass was broken and suspected home invasion. He took his son Evan across the street to a neighbour's house. David came back to the house to check on Belinda.

2015: A new hearing

In 2015, Temple's appellate attorneys secured a new hearing for the case after a close examination of the latest findings. 2016: Case overturned

In November 2016, David was an elated man when the Texas Court of Criminal Appeals overturned his conviction. On December 28, 2016, he was released from prison on bond. His brothers and parents were waiting outside to welcome him.

Belinda's family members were not happy with the ruling and said they still believed that David was guilty of Belinda's murder.

2019: Jury founds David guilty of Belinda's murder the second time

In June 2019, Temple's retrial got underway. Four days into the trial, Temple's wife Heather filed for divorce from Temple, citing a "conflict of personalities" in her marriage. The new prosecutor told the jury the break-in details on the day of the fateful incident appeared staged.
