Tracee Ellis Ross, 50, flaunts physique as she poses topless at designer fashion show in Paris

Tracee Ellis Ross shared a topless mirror selfie from when she had a fitting backstage at the Schiaparelli fashion show in Paris last week.

The American actress was attending the fashion show in Paris where she was seated front row with rapper Cardi B.

The 50-year-old wore a matching two-toned trouser suit as she wowed in the half cream half blue ensemble, sharing pictures of the moment on Instagram on Tuesday.

Contrasting the risque nude snap she uploaded for her fans, Tracee then covered up for the event as she wore the shirt buttoned up to her neck. 

The daughter of Diana Ross accessorised her outfit with chunky white framed sunglasses, heavy gold earring and long white nails.

Tracee shared plenty of content from the night on her story, including a candid shot of herself laughing heartily. 

The snap was caught by Elle Canada who tagged the star writing '@traceeellisross always the life of the party'.

In another video she embraced with Creative Director of Schiaparelli, Daniel Roseberry. 

Tracee seemed emotional as gushed about how beautiful his work was and the two exchanged some heartfelt words. 

The actress previously spoke on the Today Show earlier this year about turning 50 and how she isn't going to slow down with age. 

'This feels like a wonderful new chapter. A lot of it was brewing, and the seeds had been planted during my pre-50 years, during my time on Black-ish. Obviously, Pattern is now three years old.'

She continued: 'But for me, it's really about exploring who I am, and I couldn't be happier.'

As well as acting, Tracee is passionate about her hair product brand, Pattern. 

Tracee launched Pattern in 2019 in celebration of Black Beauty and after years of struggling to find the right way to style and use heat on her hair.

On the website she wrote: 'This has been 20 years of dreaming, 10 years in the making (I wrote my first brand pitch in 2008 right after Girlfriends), 2 years of working with chemists, and 74 samples later.' 

Her recent series Hair Tales which can be viewed on Hulu, tells the story of Black women and their humanity told through the metaphor of their hair.
